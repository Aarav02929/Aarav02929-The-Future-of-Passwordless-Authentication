Why is Passwordless Authentication Gaining Traction?/Real-World Example

1. Security Benefits

  • Reduces Phishing and Credential Theft: Without passwords, attackers cannot steal them via phishing emails or brute force attacks.
  • Eliminates Password Leaks: No passwords stored means no risks of breaches leaking sensitive credentials.

2. Improved User Experience

  • No need to remember complex passwords or reset forgotten ones.
  • Faster login process using biometrics, passkeys, or devices.

Support for Modern Technology

  • Works seamlessly with smartphones, hardware keys, and identity verification tools.
  • Supports FIDO2 and WebAuthn, enabling widespread adoption.

Compliance and Adoption

  • Passwordless solutions align with increasing regulatory demands for secure authentication (e.g., GDPR, NIST).
  • Companies like Microsoft, Google, and Apple are pushing passwordless technologies to enhance security.

Real-World Example:

  • Passkeys: Modern solutions like Apple's iCloud Keychain and Google's authentication rely on public-key cryptography to securely verify identity without using a password.
  • Passkeys are modern passwordless solutions that use public-key cryptography for authentication.
  • Apple’s iCloud Keychain and Google’s Passkey Authentication allow users to log in securely using a cryptographic key stored on their device.
  • The public key is shared with the server, while the private key remains securely stored on the user’s device.

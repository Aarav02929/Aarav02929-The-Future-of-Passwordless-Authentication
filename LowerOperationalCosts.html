Lower Operational Costs

Passwordless authentication offers significant cost savings for organizations by reducing password-related inefficiencies and support overhead. This translates to improved productivity, streamlined workflows, and better resource allocation. Here's a deeper look:

Reduced IT Support for Password Resets

  • The Hidden Cost of Password Resets:
  • Password resets are one of the most common IT support issues in organizations. Research suggests:
  • 20-50% of help desk tickets are password-related.
  • Each password reset costs an organization between $15 to $70 on average, depending on company size and complexity.
  • Why Are Password Resets Expensive?
  • Direct Costs: IT staff spend time assisting users through reset processes.
  • Indirect Costs: Lost productivity as employees are locked out and cannot work.
  • Example: A large enterprise with 10,000 employees might experience hundreds of password reset requests per month, costing thousands of dollars.
  • How Passwordless Systems Solve This:
  • By eliminating passwords, the need for resets disappears. Instead, users authenticate via:
  • Biometric scans (e.g., fingerprints, facial recognition).
  • Hardware tokens (e.g., YubiKey) or passkeys.
  • Email-based or app-based verification methods.
  • This shift reduces reliance on IT help desks, allowing support teams to focus on critical tasks like security management and infrastructure upgrades.

  • Increased Productivity

  • Password Fatigue Slows Down Work:
  • Time lost adds up quickly: A user spending even 5 minutes per week on password issues translates to hours of wasted productivity annually.
  • Faster Login Experience:
  • Passwordless systems replace typing passwords with quick and seamless authentication methods:
  • Biometrics: Fingerprint or face recognition logins take seconds.
  • Hardware Security Keys: Tapping a physical token ensures instant access.
  • One-Time Codes or Passkeys: Easy-to-enter verification codes save time compared to forgotten password workflows.
  • Real-Life Example:
  • A company implementing passwordless authentication for 1,000 employees can save hundreds of cumulative hours annually, leading to:
  • Faster onboarding for new employees.
  • Fewer disruptions in workflows.
  • Greater employee satisfaction due to a streamlined login experience.
  • Savings on Security Management
  • Reduced Password-Related Threat Mitigation Costs:
  • Organizations invest heavily in tools and resources to combat password-related attacks, such as:
  • Phishing protection.
  • Account lockouts and monitoring systems.
  • Training users on creating and managing secure passwords.

    • Passwordless authentication eliminates this risk, reducing costs for security tools and training programs.

  • Lower Compliance Costs:
  • Passwordless systems align with modern security standards (e.g., FIDO2, WebAuthn) and help organizations meet regulatory compliance (GDPR, NIST).
  • Compliance is easier to maintain since password storage and associated risks are removed.
  • Long-Term Cost Efficiency
  • Although implementing passwordless systems may require an initial investment (e.g., hardware tokens, software integration), the long-term savings far outweigh the costs:
  • Fewer IT resources spent on support.
  • Reduced security expenses.
  • Improved productivity across teams.
  • Example: Microsoft reported a significant drop in password reset requests and support tickets after adopting passwordless authentication for its employees, resulting in major cost savings.
  • Conclusion
  • Passwordless authentication not only strengthens security but also delivers measurable cost benefits for organizations:
  • Reduced reliance on IT support for password resets.
  • Improved employee productivity due to faster, seamless logins.
  • Lower security management and compliance costs.
  • By investing in passwordless solutions, businesses can achieve a higher ROI while creating a more efficient and secure work environment.
